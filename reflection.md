# Reflection — CYA-02

For this experience, I built on my initial internship search from CYA-01 by refining my strategy, improving my application materials, and approaching the process with greater intentionality. Instead of repeating the same approach, I focused on identifying weaknesses in my previous process and making targeted improvements.

One of the most important changes I made was implementing a structured application tracking system from the beginning. In CYA-01, I started tracking too late, which made it difficult to manage follow-ups and stay organized. In this iteration, I tracked every application, status update, and note in real time. This improved my efficiency and allowed me to approach the process more professionally.

I also refined how I presented my technical work. From my previous experience, I learned that specificity and measurable impact significantly strengthen applications. As a result, I improved how I described my project, TruckTap, by emphasizing metrics, system design decisions, and real-world impact. This made my applications more compelling and aligned with what recruiters look for in backend engineering candidates.

Additionally, I became more strategic in targeting roles. Rather than applying broadly, I prioritized positions that closely matched my backend-focused skill set, including Django, REST APIs, and systems-oriented work. This allowed me to focus on quality over quantity and better align my efforts with my long-term career goals.

I worked independently throughout this process and took full ownership of improving my approach. My contributions included refining my resume, updating application materials, maintaining a structured tracker, and making more informed decisions about where to apply.

I assess my performance as improved compared to my initial experience. I demonstrated stronger organization, clearer communication of my technical skills, and more strategic decision-making. However, I recognize that I can continue improving by starting earlier, increasing networking efforts, and practicing technical interviews more consistently.

This experience further strengthened my alignment with my career goals of becoming a backend engineer. It helped me better understand real hiring pipelines, improve how I present my work, and develop a more disciplined and professional approach to career development. Overall, this iteration moved me closer to securing an internship and becoming a more competitive candidate.
