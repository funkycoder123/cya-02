# Reflection — CYA-02

I built off my first internship search process, CYA-01, by reassessing my past process and becoming more deliberate in my efforts. I tried to avoid repeating a lot of CYA-01 as is and instead find my low hanging fruits for improvement. I identified a few problems and system improvements with how I had searched previously, and iterated off of this.

I set up a process to track all of my applications early in the process. I had a problem in CYA-01 of tracking too late in the application process. There were a lot of “unknowns” and I felt behind when it came to follow ups. I created a template that allowed me to track each application, note status changes and log any notes throughout. This system was highly effective and improved my efficiency and professionalism in approaching this process.

I started describing my technical work with greater specificity. After CYA-01, I realized that any specificity and quantifiable impact is great for applications. I noticed that when I measured my work and referenced architecture decisions and optimizations, that made the work sound more legitimate in recruiters’ ears. I improved how I described my capstone project, TruckTap, by including metrics and system design decisions and gave context of what work had real world impact.

I more deliberately targeted the roles I was applying for. I made an effort to be more strategic in my role selection. In the past, I applied more broadly and to a large volume of positions. I made an effort to curate the positions that best aligned with my skillset and career goals, and focused on backend focused roles, leveraging my skills in Django, REST APIs, and general systems-oriented work.

I was fully self-directed in this process, and was responsible for all of my contributions to this project. I built off my prior knowledge to re-format my resume, curate materials to apply to roles, use a tracking system for all applications, and make more informed decisions about role selection.

Overall, I would rate this project as a net improvement from my last experience searching. I felt stronger in my organization, communication of technical ability, and decision making. I think this is an area of continuous improvement, and while this process was a successful iteration, there’s a lot of room for continued improvement. I think I could start earlier to give myself more time in this process, I could spend more time networking and reaching out to others, and I should spend more time practicing technical interviews.

This project aligned strongly with my career goals of becoming a backend engineer. It helped me more tangibly understand a real hiring pipeline, describe and convey my work in more effective ways, and helped me further refine a more disciplined and professional approach to career development and job searching.
